Sound

Tracing an arc from early 80s new wave and post-punk sounds to the present, Ones&Zeros combine dual male/female vocals with sharp, fuzzed-out guitar riffs that can veer off into folk, country, psychedelic and, sometimes, orchestral territories.

Ones&Zeros is an indie rock band from Vancouver, BC, Canada that plays gritty, distorted pop songs that can trace a lineage back to the anger, dissatisfaction, experimentation and exuberance that was brewing in the late 70s & early 80s music scenes in the UK and NYC. These scenes dared to raise a ruckus and gave rise to some of the most important sounds of our day: power-pop; punk; post-punk and new wave, among others.

Ones&Zeros takes inspiration from these times to complain and holler about circumstances that are unique to our generation. The band’s sound is no one-trick pony though. It’s not all about setting the amps to 11 and putting the pedal to the metal. Some songs will kick out the jams with slashing, distorted riffs and frenetic drumming to be sure, but others can be stripped bare to their soiled, acoustic undergarments. Yet, whether lurching from light to shade, or from loud to quiet and back to loud again, the band usually takes pains to sneak in a few jabs about where we’re headed in these turbulent times.

History

Ones&Zeros initially took shape as a bedroom recording project for Jason Maxwell in 2012. After having been on a forced exile from music for what seemed like ages, he returned to it that year on a whim and was shocked to have a steady glut of songs pour forth. Feeling it would be a shame to consign such an unlikely stash of music to soon-to-be-forgotten notebooks and digital music files, he resolved to share it with friends and set out to hack together a band later that year.

By year’s end, Ones&Zeros had been born and a 5-song EP had been recorded and would be released in June of 2013. The self-titled release was produced by Ty Berrie and the band and was mastered by Carl Saff in Chicago, IL. The members at this time included: Caroline Dawid (vocals); Jason (vocals, guitars, keyboards), Darius Minwalla (drums) and Jesse Lyon (bass).

Later that year, the membership evolved into its current form. Plans were also laid for a second, follow-up EP (“The City Don’t Care”) slated for release in June 2014.

Music In Our Times

At a time when music sales and attention spans are plummeting precipitously, can new bands have a hope in hell of existing beyond the blink of an eye?

Probably not.

But we’re going to thumb our noses at common sense and throw out some music into the ether anyway. Thanks for stopping by and having a listen.
